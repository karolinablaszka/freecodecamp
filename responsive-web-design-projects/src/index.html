About us

We are a young scientific club acting at the Faculty of Polish and Classical Philology, also associating students from other faculties (Faculty of Social Sciences, Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science). Our goal is primarily to explore the language and culture of people with hearing impairment, break stereotypes and strive for mutual integration. We wonder what the difference is between the System Sign Language and Polish Sign Language and how development and education in the field of Polish for deaf people takes place. We are looking for inspiring people who change the world with their open and positive attitude, we pass on their stories. We ask questions about whether hearing culture can occur next to the Deaf.

Our effects

The effects of our work with Telewizja akademicka UAM.TV, Karolina Blaszka, Karolina Ruta, Maria Skoczyńska, Eliza Szygenda and Marta Górska!
We celebrated International Day for the Deaf. An opportunity like this gives us a chance to raise an awareness that being a hard of hearing person is sometimes invisible. They also have needs which some of us do not realize. Basically, our actions aim to make the society aware about difficulties which deaf/hard of hearing people struggle with, without significant effects. We would like to make better our world for people with such disabilities. We have been organizing Polish sign languages courses since several years for students as well as for lecturers. Besides, we did some workshops and events which helped so much! We already noticed that our actions engaged the attention of the whole university. Some things have started to change. There are a lot of people willing to take part in these courses and to cooperate with us. We are very happy for that! In addition, it motivates us to continue our work and to broaden horizons ‘till the cows come home’. :) Don’t be deaf to the needs of the deaf!
